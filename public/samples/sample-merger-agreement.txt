MERGER AGREEMENT

AGREEMENT AND PLAN OF MERGER

This Agreement and Plan of Merger ("Agreement") is entered into as of October 15, 2024, by and among TechCorp Industries, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Parent"), Acquisition Sub LLC, a Delaware limited liability company and wholly owned subsidiary of Parent ("Merger Sub"), and InnovateTech Solutions, Inc., a Missouri corporation ("Company").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the respective Boards of Directors of Parent, Merger Sub, and Company have approved this Agreement and the transactions contemplated hereby;

WHEREAS, Parent desires to acquire Company through a merger of Merger Sub with and into Company;

WHEREAS, the merger will result in significant operational synergies and cost savings estimated at $12.5 million annually;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1. THE MERGER

1.1 Merger. Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, at the Effective Time, Merger Sub shall be merged with and into Company, and Company shall be the surviving corporation.

1.2 Effects of Merger. The merger shall have the effects set forth in Section 351 of the Delaware General Corporation Law and Section 351.455 of the Missouri Business Corporation Act.

1.3 Certificate of Incorporation and Bylaws. The Certificate of Incorporation and Bylaws of Company shall be amended and restated in their entirety as set forth in Exhibit A.

2. CONSIDERATION

2.1 Merger Consideration. Each share of Company Common Stock issued and outstanding immediately prior to the Effective Time shall be converted into the right to receive $47.50 in cash ("Per Share Price").

2.2 Total Consideration. The aggregate merger consideration is approximately $285 million based on 6 million outstanding shares.

2.3 Payment. Payment shall be made through Parent's designated paying agent within five (5) business days of the closing date.

3. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES OF COMPANY

3.1 Corporate Authorization. Company has full corporate power and authority to execute and deliver this Agreement.

3.2 Financial Statements. The audited financial statements of Company as of December 31, 2023, fairly present the financial condition of Company.

3.3 Material Contracts. Schedule 3.3 contains a complete list of all material contracts to which Company is a party.

3.4 Litigation. There is no pending or threatened litigation against Company that would have a material adverse effect.

3.5 Intellectual Property. Company owns or has valid licenses to all intellectual property necessary for its business operations.

3.6 Employee Benefits. All employee benefit plans are listed in Schedule 3.6 and are in compliance with applicable law.

4. CONDITIONS TO CLOSING

4.1 Regulatory Approvals. All required regulatory approvals, including HSR clearance, shall have been obtained.

4.2 Shareholder Approval. Company shareholders shall have approved this Agreement by the required vote.

4.3 No Material Adverse Change. Company shall not have experienced any material adverse change since the date of this Agreement.

4.4 Legal Opinions. Each party shall have received satisfactory legal opinions from their respective counsel.

5. TERMINATION

5.1 Mutual Consent. This Agreement may be terminated at any time by mutual written consent of the parties.

5.2 Breach. Either party may terminate if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within thirty (30) days.

5.3 Regulatory Denial. Either party may terminate if required regulatory approvals are denied.

5.4 Termination Fee. If Company terminates to accept a superior proposal, Company shall pay Parent a termination fee of $8.5 million.

6. INDEMNIFICATION

6.1 Survival. The representations and warranties shall survive closing for eighteen (18) months.

6.2 Indemnification Cap. Total indemnification liability shall not exceed $28.5 million (10% of transaction value).

6.3 Escrow. $14.25 million shall be held in escrow for indemnification claims.

7. EMPLOYEE MATTERS

7.1 Employment Continuation. Parent shall offer employment to all Company employees for at least twelve (12) months post-closing.

7.2 Benefits Continuation. Employee benefits shall be maintained at substantially similar levels for twenty-four (24) months.

7.3 Severance. Enhanced severance packages are provided for key executives as detailed in Schedule 7.3.

8. MISCELLANEOUS

8.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by Delaware law.

8.2 Jurisdiction. Exclusive jurisdiction in Delaware Court of Chancery.

8.3 Expenses. Each party shall bear its own transaction expenses, estimated at $2.3 million for Company.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.
